# FreeCapT — brand package (v0.1)

**Date:** 2026-05-27
**Audience:** Founding team, future hires (designer, marketing, sales), any agency or contractor doing brand or copy work

This is the working brand foundation. It captures positioning, voice, visual direction, and a copy library. It is opinionated — every guideline below is a decision, not a suggestion. If something below stops fitting reality, we update it; we don't drift away from it silently.

---

## 1. Name

**Wordmark:** `FreeCapT`

Style conventions (always followed):
- Internal cap: F, C, T are capitalized. F-r-e-e-C-a-p-T.
- Never `FREECAPT` (all caps), never `Freecapt` (sentence case), never `Free Cap T` (with spaces), never `freecapt` outside of URLs.
- In running prose: `FreeCapT` (e.g., "FreeCapT is free for personal cap tables").
- In URLs and addresses: `freecapt.com`, `support@freecapt.com`. Lowercase URLs are conventional and we follow that.

**Pronunciation:** *free-cap-T* — three syllables. The "T" is pronounced as the letter, not collapsed into "capt."

**Etymology / what it means:** the name says what we are. *Free* is the wedge (Carta is famously not). *CapT* is "Cap Table" shorthand familiar to any founder who has ever opened a spreadsheet labeled "captable.xlsx". The name is descriptive on purpose — there is zero brand interpretation cost. A founder seeing "FreeCapT" understands the product within two seconds.

## 2. Positioning

**One-line positioning (the elevator):**
> The free cap table for European founders.

**Slightly fuller (a sentence for the hero):**
> FreeCapT is the cap table you can build, share, and trust — for free, in your language, under your country's rules. We charge $15/month when you want AI, polished exports, and the stakeholder portal. We never charge by stakeholder.

**Three-sentence pitch:**
> Carta was built for venture-backed US startups and prices accordingly — $2,400+ per year for a tiny company. Most founders don't need that complexity. FreeCapT is a free, AI-native cap table built for the 95% of private companies that aren't VC-backed unicorns — bootstrapped startups, family businesses, agencies, holding companies, small SaaS — across seven jurisdictions including Denmark, Norway, Sweden, Germany, Switzerland, the UK and US. Pay $15/month only when you want to share with stakeholders, generate legal-grade documents, or use the AI features.

**What we are not:**
- We are not Carta. We are not for venture-backed scaleups with 5 preferred classes.
- We are not a SAFE marketplace, a secondary trading platform, or a fund admin tool.
- We are not US-first with European bolt-ons. The European jurisdictions are first-class.
- We are not legalese pretending to be friendly. We are friendly *because* we hide the legalese.

## 3. Audience

**Tier 1 ICP (primary, in order of strategic importance):**

1. **Nordic bootstrapped founders** — solo or small team, DK ApS / NO AS / SE AB, building a business they intend to own. The beachhead.
2. **DACH SMB founders** — German GmbH, Swiss AG/GmbH. Often family or partner businesses. Notary-fatigued.
3. **UK Ltd. founders** — first-time founders running EMI schemes, intimidated by the HMRC complexity.
4. **US Delaware C-Corp + LLC founders** — bootstrapped or pre-seed. The "I might raise but haven't yet" demographic.
5. **Family businesses, agencies, holding companies, small real estate structures** — owners who need an ownership system but were never the target of Carta's sales pitch.

**Tier 2 (secondary):**

1. **Angel investors and advisors** — they enter through stakeholder portal, see the portfolio dashboard, become founder customers when they start their own thing.
2. **Accountants and Treuhänder serving SMB** — they manage 10–30 client cap tables; we save them spreadsheet hell.

**Not us, at all:**

- Series B+ scaleups with five preferred classes
- Public companies, late-stage pre-IPO, large funds
- Carta enterprise customers (we are not winning them; they have lawyers)

## 4. Voice and tone

**Voice (always):**
- **Direct.** Nordic-honest. We say what we mean. No salesy phrases like "unlock your potential" or "supercharge your equity."
- **Founder-empathetic.** We know cap tables are scary because lawyers and Carta made them scary. We don't pretend they aren't.
- **Quietly confident.** We don't shout about being free; we make it obvious and let the reader feel smart for noticing.
- **Plain.** A normal person reads our copy without a dictionary. Tax jargon goes in tooltips, never headlines.
- **Anti-establishment but not bitter.** We're not against Carta as a villain; they served their audience well. We just have a different one.

**Tone shifts by context:**

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Landing page hero | Confident, plainspoken, slightly bold |
| Product UI labels | Brief, neutral, technically precise |
| AI explanations to founders | Warm, plain-English, occasionally a bit witty |
| Stakeholder welcome emails | Genuinely warm, never corporate |
| Legal documents we generate | Precise and formal — match the jurisdiction's expectations |
| Help docs / FAQ | Patient, anticipating the obvious next question |
| Error messages | Specific, blame-free, points at the fix |
| Pricing page | Direct, no marketing footnotes, no asterisks |
| Trust / security page | Sober, specific, technically credible |

**Words we use:** stakeholder, grant, vest, share class, country pack, founder, the ledger, your data.

**Words we avoid:**
- "Equity management platform" — we are a cap table tool.
- "Solution" — we make products.
- "Empower," "unlock," "supercharge," "revolutionize," "disrupt" — bro-SaaS lexicon.
- "Best-in-class" — meaningless.
- "Game-changer," "next-generation" — empty.
- "Industry-leading" — we don't lead an industry; we serve a market.

**Specific phrasing decisions:**
- We say "cap table," not "capitalization table" (the latter only on legal exports).
- We say "free" as a description of the product, not "freemium" (sounds gimmicky).
- We say "Paid plan" and "$15/month," not "Pro tier" or "Premium." Plain.
- We say "magic link," not "passwordless authentication" or "one-click sign-in."

## 5. Visual identity

### 5.1 Logo

**Wordmark only at launch.** A typeset `FreeCapT` in Inter Display or similar geometric sans-serif, set in slate-900 with the `C` of "Cap" optionally in emerald-600 to add a single visual hook without complicating the mark.

Concept for an eventual icon mark (decided later, not v1): a stylized stripe or stacked-bar form suggesting a cap table — the kind of horizontal-bar visual that says "ownership distribution" without being literal.

**Don'ts:**
- No graphic of a literal cap (the hat). We chose this name and we live with the pun being subtle.
- No globe (multi-jurisdiction is a feature, not an identity).
- No abstract flowing-data swoosh.

### 5.2 Color palette (extends v0.6 decision)

| Role | Color | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary text / chrome** | Slate 900 | `#0F172A` | Body text, headers, dominant UI |
| **Background** | White | `#FFFFFF` | App canvas |
| **Surface** | Slate 50 | `#F8FAFC` | Subtle panels, app backdrop |
| **Border** | Slate 200 | `#E2E8F0` | Quiet dividers, table rules |
| **Primary action** | Emerald 600 | `#059669` | Buttons, active states, brand accents |
| **Primary action hover** | Emerald 700 | `#047857` | Hover/active darker tone |
| **Soft accent backdrop** | Emerald 50 | `#ECFDF5` | Tinted callouts, recommended-option highlights |
| **Warning / pending** | Amber 500/600 | `#F59E0B` / `#D97706` | Pending signatures, deadline warnings, SAFE flags |
| **Danger** | Red 600 | `#DC2626` | Destructive actions only |
| **Muted text** | Slate 500 | `#64748B` | Secondary copy, captions |

Color discipline rule: **emerald is for action and the brand mark. Amber is for "needs attention." Red is for destructive only.** Tables and lists are slate-on-white with no decorative color.

### 5.3 Typography

| Use | Typeface | Weight / size |
|---|---|---|
| Display headlines (landing) | **Inter Display** | 700, fluid 48–72px |
| Body headlines (in-app, doc h1/h2) | **Inter** | 600, 24–32px |
| Body text | **Inter** | 400, 15–16px, 1.5 line height |
| UI labels / buttons | **Inter** | 500, 13–14px |
| Tabular numbers (cap table, financial) | **Inter (tabular-nums)** | 400, monospaced numerals for alignment |
| Monospace (code, IDs, timestamps) | **JetBrains Mono** | 400, 12–13px |

Inter is the obvious right answer for this product — wide language coverage (covers all five launch UI languages including German umlauts and Nordic ø/å/æ correctly), broad weight range, free, well-loved by financial SaaS. JetBrains Mono is the cleanest free monospace.

### 5.4 Iconography

- Simple line icons, 1.5px stroke, slate-700.
- Lucide Icons (https://lucide.dev) — free, comprehensive, design-language-consistent with the type and the wireframes.
- No custom icon set in v1. Reach for product-specific custom icons only when no Lucide option fits.

### 5.5 Imagery

- **Minimal photography at launch.** If any: real founders, real cap tables (anonymized if needed), real screens — never stock photos.
- Hero illustrations are simple, geometric, derived from the cap-table donut shape. Slate + emerald, single style.
- No 3D renders, no "isometric SaaS scenes," no abstract gradient blobs. The aesthetic is precise and serious — financial.

### 5.6 Logo / brand placement rules

- Logo always slate-900 unless on dark background, then white.
- Minimum clear space around the wordmark equals the height of the cap-T.
- Minimum size: 80px wide (web), 24mm wide (print).

### 6.5 Value-prop micro-copy (the three things to repeat everywhere)

1. **Free for your personal cap table** — no asterisk.

When direct comparison helps:
- "Carta starts at $2,400/year. We're free."
- "Carta charges by stakeholder. We don't."
- "Carta is built for US scaleups. We're built for the rest."
- "Carta needs your lawyer. FreeCapT doesn't."

Rule: name Carta no more than once per page. Beyond that, it's punching down.

### 6.7 Trust copy (for security/footer)

- 🇪🇺 GDPR-compliant. EU and US data regions, your choice.
- 🔒 AES-256 encrypted. TLS 1.3. SOC 2 in progress.
- 📋 Audit log of every state change.
- 💾 Your data is yours. One-click export.

### 6.8 Empty states

- (Cap table, no data): *"Let's get [Company] set up. The fastest way is to paste from a spreadsheet."*
- (Documents, no data): *"Drop a PDF here, or generate one from our jurisdiction templates."*
- (Stakeholders, no data): *"Add everyone at once — paste, type, or upload. Singular adds are below."*

### 6.9 Error messages (pattern)

- Specific (what went wrong)
- Blame-free (no "you did X")
- Pointing at the fix (what to try)

Example: *"This grant exceeds the EMI per-employee lifetime limit of £250,000. Switch to an unapproved option grant, or split into multiple grants over time."*
Not: *"Error: validation failed. Please contact support."*

## 7. Marketing assets and rollout

### 7.1 Domain plan

- **freecapt.com** — primary marketing site (this brand package + landing page).
- **app.freecapt.com** — the product app.
- **admin.freecapt.com** — the internal staff app (§5.20).
- **status.freecapt.com** — public status page.
- **docs.freecapt.com** — help docs (post-launch).
- Country variants: **freecapt.com/da**, **/no**, **/sv**, **/de**, **/en** — language switches on the same domain, not separate ccTLDs. Cheaper, simpler SEO, single brand.

### 7.2 Landing page architecture (v1)

For launch, single-page homepage with anchor scroll. Sections:

1. **Hero** — headline, sub, two CTAs (primary "Get started — free," secondary "See pricing").
2. **What it is** — three value props (the §6.5 micro-copy expanded into three icon cards).
3. **Country packs grid** — visual showing the seven flags and entity types we support, with a "free in your country" message.
4. **Product preview** — animated or scrolling preview of the cap table screen + AI assistant + stakeholder portal.
5. **AI features** — short callout: extraction at setup, ask-your-cap-table chat, explain-this buttons.
6. **Round modeling** — the new paid feature gets its own short section with a visual.
7. **Stakeholder portal** — the second-product-surface story (employees see their stake, plain English, free portfolio view).
8. **Pricing** — the §5.10 table verbatim. Two columns. No third tier confusion.
9. **Trust** — security badges + the four §6.7 trust statements.
10. **FAQ** — 6–8 questions: "Is it really free?" "What jurisdictions?" "Do I need a lawyer?" "How is this different from Carta?" "Is my data safe?" "What about GDPR?"
11. **Final CTA** — same primary as hero, with the language-detection note ("Available in Dansk, Norsk, Svenska, Deutsch, English").
12. **Footer** — links, security, status, docs, company.

Post-launch additions (not v1):
- Per-country landing pages for SEO (`/dk/ejertabel`, `/de/cap-table-gmbh`, etc.) — each tells the same story in the local jurisdiction's vocabulary.
- Comparison pages ("FreeCapT vs Carta," "FreeCapT vs Ledgy") — important once we have customer proof points.
- Customer case studies — once we have customers willing.
- Blog / changelog at /changelog.

### 7.3 Social and outreach

- **LinkedIn** as primary professional channel. Nicolai's existing network + Nordic SaaS founder community.
- **Twitter/X** as secondary — useful for US founder community, the "indie hacker" demographic.
- No TikTok, no Instagram, no Facebook ads in v1. Wrong demographic for SMB cap tables.
- Newsletter via the help docs / changelog — opt-in only, monthly product update + one piece of educational content (e.g., "Understanding EMI for UK founders").

### 7.4 PR / launch sequence (rough)

- **Phase 0 (now):** brand and landing page live. Closed beta with 10–20 friendly founders in the Nordic network.
- **Phase 1 (closed beta, ~6–8 weeks):** invite-only signups. Polish onboarding, fix the things that break.
- **Phase 2 (public launch):** Product Hunt + a press push aimed at Nordic/European tech publications (Sifted, Tech.eu, Børsen, Shifter, Di Digital). Carta-comparison angle. Founder story.
- **Phase 3 (post-launch growth):** customer-story posts, jurisdiction-specific landing pages, content marketing.

## 8. Brand "don'ts" (the bright lines)

- **Don't trash competitors gratuitously.** One Carta mention per page max; always factual and specific.
- **Don't promise SOC 2 / ISO before we have them.** Our roadmap is public; we don't fudge.
- **Don't gate the basic value behind email walls.** "Sign up to see pricing" / "Talk to sales" — never on FreeCapT.
- **Don't use stock photos of diverse-looking-people-laughing-at-laptops.** We don't.
- **Don't claim "AI-powered" without listing the specific AI features.** Vague AI claims age badly.
- **Don't add emojis in product UI** beyond country flags and the few cases that aid scanability (✓ ⧗ ⚠). Marketing site can use them sparingly; product UI is restrained.
- **Don't run countdown timers or "limited time" pricing.** Cheap, undermines trust.
- **Don't use dark patterns.** Cancellation is one click. Downgrade is one click. Data export is one click. We are not in the SaaS-trap business.

## 9. Living-document note

This brand package is v0.1 and will evolve. The things most likely to change in the first six months:

- **Copy refinement** based on what converts (A/B testing in landing pages).
- **Visual identity polish** — when we hire a designer, the logo and illustration system get a real treatment.
- **Voice calibration in stakeholder-facing copy** — early users will tell us what feels right.
- **Country-specific voice variations** — Danish, German, etc., copy by native speakers, not translations from English.

The things that should *not* change without strong reason:

- The name and the wordmark style
- The positioning around SMB / European / feature-based free
- The slate + emerald palette
- The "no asterisks, no dark patterns" stance
- The plain-language rule
